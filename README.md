# Desire and the Daemon

## Graduate Students as Produced Subjects: Imaginaries, Desire, and Becoming

This project is not a static website.
It is a **screened performance**.

It explores graduate education not as a neutral pathway toward mastery, but as a site where subjects are produced—through institutional imaginaries, disciplinary expectations, and the shaping of desire.

Graduate students emerge here as contradictory figures:
necessary and disposable, expert and imposter, visible and unseen, becoming and not-yet.

Rather than presenting a linear argument, this project stages those contradictions.

You will not move through this space in a straight line.
That is intentional.

Each section—Imaginaries, Produced Subjects, Desire, Discipline, Liminality, Becoming—offers an entry point into the conditions that shape graduate student life. These are not stable categories. They overlap, interrupt, and reconfigure one another.

Some elements will feel incomplete.
Some connections will not resolve immediately.
Some meanings may only emerge after you leave.

This is not failure.
This is the work of becoming.

The screen here does not simply display content.
It participates in meaning-making—shaping what can be seen, felt, and known.

This project invites you to move, pause, return, and reconsider.

Not to master the argument—
but to encounter it.

---

## Structure

* **Imaginaries** – Who graduate school imagines students to be
* **Produced Subjects** – How institutions construct and position graduate students
* **Desire** – The force that drives, sustains, and exceeds institutional control
* **Discipline** – The mechanisms that shape, constrain, and redirect desire
* **Liminality** – The unstable, in-between condition of graduate student life
* **Becoming** – The ongoing, unfinished process of identity formation
