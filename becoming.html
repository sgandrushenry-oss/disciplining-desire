Becoming

Graduate education does not culminate in a finished subject. It moves through unfinishedness, relation, uncertainty, and change. Becoming is not a final stage after contradiction. It is what remains possible within it. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}
The point is not arrival. The point is learning how to remain in motion.
This page stays quieter because becoming is not resolution. It is ongoing, relational, and not fully capturable in advance.

You do not leave graduate school as a completed version of yourself. If anything, you may leave more aware of unfinishedness, contradiction, and the limits of certainty.

Becoming does not erase instability. It learns how to live with it differently.

Becoming is not mastery. It is the capacity to continue even when mastery does not arrive.
Becoming is not coherence. It allows for contradiction, misfit, return, revision, and partial understanding.
Becoming is not sameness. It does not require replication of the institution, the mentor, or the field’s dominant image of itself.
Becoming is relational. It emerges through writing, teaching, conversation, risk, interruption, and response.

Graduate students do not need certainty.

They need support in navigating uncertainty. Not because uncertainty is a flaw, but because it is already one of the conditions they inhabit. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

To become otherwise is not to arrive somewhere pure, outside power, outside institutions, outside difficulty.

It is to keep open the possibility that other forms of intellectual, relational, and institutional life might still be made. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
