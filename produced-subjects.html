Produced Subjects

Graduate students are not only students in graduate school. They are also produced there—through institutional expectations, disciplinary norms, evaluation, labor, and time.
Necessary
Needed to teach, write, assist, sustain, and produce.
Disposable
Always provisional. Always replaceable.
Expert
Expected to perform knowledge, authority, and competence.
Imposter
Reminded, subtly or directly, of insufficiency and not-yet-ness.
Visible
Counted, evaluated, measured, and made legible through performance.
Unseen
Histories, limits, attachments, labor, and needs often disappear.
Stable enough to labor. Unstable enough to be denied security.
Hover and contradiction structure the page because contradiction structures the subject.
They are told to become. But not always given the conditions that make becoming livable.
They are expected to know. Even while learning remains partial, deferred, and in motion.
They are trained to participate. Yet the terms of participation are already shaped in advance.
They are produced as subjects who must manage contradiction quietly. Professional, but not fully recognized. Present, but not secure. Becoming, but not arrived.
