Imaginaries

Graduate school begins before you arrive.

It begins in what it imagines you to be.

You are expected to move forward.

On time. Without interruption. Without complication.

Student.

Traditional. Funded. Focused. Available.

But what if you arrive already carrying something else?

Work. Family. Debt. History. Time.

The institution does not always see that.

Or it sees—but does not account for it.

You begin inside an expectation.

And then you try to become within it.
